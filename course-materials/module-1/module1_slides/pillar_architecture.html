Repository Architecture: The Foundation of Scalable Project Organization

Repository Architecture establishes clear, logical structure that scales with project complexity and team growth.
A well-architected repository includes: clear directory structure, consistent naming conventions, appropriate .gitignore configuration, and branch protection rules.
Poor architecture creates technical debt that compounds over time—costing teams 20-30% of development time in maintenance and refactoring.
Professional architecture decisions made at project inception prevent costly refactoring later and establish standards for the entire team.
This pillar ensures repositories remain navigable and maintainable as teams and codebases grow, preventing chaos and confusion.
